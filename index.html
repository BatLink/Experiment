Experiment

Thank you for participating

In this task, you play a "social" game. you need to choose one of four social agents (A, B, C or D) with the mouse.

Selecting each agent will show his or her emotional response to you. Your task is to select the agent with the most positive emotions (most kind and pleasant).

Notice that when you select an agent there are two emotional responses: One and then another. Both of these count towards the agent’s response to you (they have the same weight).

It is not possible to know in advance what would be the agents’ emotional response to you. You will find this out during the game.

You are completely free to switch from one agent to the other in whatever frequency you want.

At the end of the game you will get money based on the positivity/negativity of the agent’s emotions as ranked by a previous panel. For example, lets say that an agent was recorded at a positivity/negativity level of 2.5 (on a scale of 1 to 5) this mean you will get 2.5 cents for each round you select the agent.

You do not know the number of rounds in advance.

Also, the computer does not change the order of the agents once the game starts or changes outcomes because of choices you made.

Press space bar to start. Good luck!
